About Me

I am a Ph.D. student in the computer vision lab at Indiana University, where I am grateful to work with Prof. David Crandall and Prof. Linda Smith.

My main research interest lies in learning through action. Particularly, in how active agency can accelerate learning by influencing the data we receive as input and vice-versa. I am currently working on embodied, self-supervised representation learning.

Prior to joining IU, I received my B.Sc. degree in Mathematics and Information Technology (double major) from Cumberland University.
